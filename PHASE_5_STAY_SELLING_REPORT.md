# Phase 5 Stay Selling Report

## Content changes
- Rewrote the room section introduction to focus on comparing layouts, bed setup, and stay style before enquiry.
- Reworked both room cards with benefit-led intros, clearer “who it suits / layout / best use” summaries, and a stronger image badge per room type.
- Added a dedicated amenities section with sharper labels, clearer practical-vs-extra grouping, and more premium, guest-relevant descriptions.
- Tightened the offers copy into four credible direct-booking benefits, then added a stronger action panel that points guests back to availability.

## Structural changes
- Inserted a new amenities section between Rooms and Offers.
- Added a room comparison cue row above the room cards to make twin-vs-double decisions faster.
- Simplified the offers section from six generic cards to four more persuasive benefit cards plus a closing call-to-action panel.
- Moved the booking section later in the main homepage flow so the page now builds desire and reassurance before asking the guest to enquire.

## Comparison improvements
- Added shared comparison cues for room size, core in-room comforts, and the key difference between Twin and Double.
- Standardized each room card around the same comparison headings:
  - who it suits
  - layout
  - best use
  - key in-room amenities
- Kept the room tags for beds, size, and guest count so guests can scan key facts quickly.

## Proof strategy
- Surfaced room size repeatedly as a practical proof point: hero proof strip, room tags, and room comparison row.
- Elevated practical reassurance around fast Wi‑Fi, parking, A/C, and hot showers inside the new amenities section.
- Kept the Google Maps review proof and ratings context in the hero, reviews, and booking flow.
- Preserved concrete guest reassurance across the page: quiet-side requests, parking subject to availability, check-in/check-out facts, and direct contact methods.

## Sections removed or tightened
- Tightened the room cards by replacing weaker generic copy with specific use-case guidance.
- Tightened the offers section by removing two lower-value cards and keeping four direct-booking benefits that better support action.
- Reduced clutter in the amenities story by separating must-have practical items from helpful extras.
