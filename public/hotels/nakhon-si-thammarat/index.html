About Nakhon Si Thammarat

Nakhon Si Thammarat is one of the oldest cities in Southern Thailand, with over 1,000 years of recorded history. It is the capital of Nakhon Si Thammarat Province, located on the Gulf of Thailand coast approximately 780 km south of Bangkok. The city is known for its Buddhist heritage, distinctive Southern Thai cuisine, traditional shadow puppet theatre (Nang Talung), and nielloware silver crafts.

Unlike the busy resort towns of Phuket or Koh Samui, Nakhon Si Thammarat is a real Thai city with a relaxed pace, authentic food, and cultural depth that rewards those who take the time to explore it.

Key Attractions Near Sandbox Hotel

  • Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan — One of the most important Buddhist temples in all of Thailand, with a towering chedi visible from across the city. Free entry.
  • Nakhon Si Thammarat National Museum — Covers the history of the southern peninsula, with artefacts, Buddha images, and shadow puppet displays.
  • Bovorn Bazaar & Night Market — The go-to spot for local street food, Southern Thai dishes, and evening browsing.
  • Old City Walking Street — Weekend heritage market with artisan stalls and local food vendors in the historic shophouse district.
  • Nang Talung Shadow Puppet Theatre — A living Southern Thai art tradition, designated as an intangible cultural heritage.
  • Khanom Beach / Sichon Beach — Gulf Coast beaches approximately 60–80 km from the city, ideal for a half-day or day trip.

How to Get to Nakhon Si Thammarat

  • By air: Nakhon Si Thammarat Airport (NST/VTSN) — daily flights from Bangkok Don Mueang and Suvarnabhumi. Approximately 1.5 hours.
  • By train: Overnight sleeper train from Bangkok Hua Lamphong or Bang Sue Grand. Approximately 12–14 hours.
  • By bus: Long-distance coaches from Bangkok's Southern Bus Terminal (Sai Tai Mai). Approximately 10–12 hours.
