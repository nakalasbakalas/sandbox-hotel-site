What is Southern Thailand?

Southern Thailand (ภาคใต้) is the southernmost region of the country, forming a narrow peninsula stretching approximately 900 km from Chumphon Province in the north to the border with Malaysia in the south. The region encompasses 14 provinces and is home to over 9 million people.

It is divided into two distinct coastlines:

Gulf of Thailand Coast (East)

Warmer, calmer water. Best visited February–September. Key destinations: Koh Samui, Koh Phangan, Koh Tao (Surat Thani), Nakhon Si Thammarat, Songkhla, Hat Yai.

Andaman Sea Coast (West)

Known for dramatic limestone cliffs and clear waters. Best visited November–April. Key destinations: Phuket, Krabi, Koh Lanta, Koh Phi Phi, Trang, Satun.

Where to Stay in Southern Thailand by Travel Style

Beach & Island Holidays

Best bases: Phuket, Krabi (Ao Nang), Koh Samui, Koh Phangan, Koh Lanta. These destinations offer the full beach resort experience with a wide range of accommodation from budget guesthouses to luxury resorts.

Cultural & Historical Travel

Best base: Nakhon Si Thammarat. One of Thailand's oldest cities (over 1,000 years of history), home to Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan, the National Museum, Nang Talung shadow puppet theatre, and traditional nielloware crafts. Sandbox Hotel is located here. Also worth visiting: Songkhla, Phatthalung.

National Parks & Nature

Best bases: Khao Lak (for Khao Sok National Park and the Similan Islands), Surat Thani (Khao Sok), Trang (Hat Chao Mai Marine National Park).

Food Travel

Southern Thai cuisine is distinctly different from Central Thai food — spicier, with richer curries, fresh seafood, and distinct influences from Malay and Indian cooking traditions. Nakhon Si Thammarat and Hat Yai are considered among the best cities for authentic Southern Thai food.

Business Travel

Best bases: Hat Yai (the commercial hub of Southern Thailand), Nakhon Si Thammarat, Surat Thani. Sandbox Hotel in Nakhon Si Thammarat is well-suited for business visitors — spacious rooms with work desks, reliable Wi-Fi, and flexible stays.

Nakhon Si Thammarat — Southern Thailand's Most Historic City

Nakhon Si Thammarat (นครศรีธรรมราช) is the capital of Nakhon Si Thammarat Province and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in Thailand. Located on the Gulf Coast approximately 780 km south of Bangkok, it has a recorded history spanning more than 1,000 years — predating Bangkok by centuries.

It is one of Southern Thailand's most underrated destinations for visitors seeking authentic Thai culture, deep Buddhist heritage, and genuine local life away from the beach resort crowds.

Key Attractions

  • Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan — one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in Southern Thailand
  • Nakhon Si Thammarat National Museum
  • Nang Talung shadow puppet theatre
  • Bovorn Bazaar & local markets
  • Khanom & Sichon beaches (day trip)

FAQ — Hotels in Southern Thailand

Where should I stay in Southern Thailand?

Depends on your purpose: beach holidays → Phuket, Krabi, or Koh Samui; island-hopping → Surat Thani; cultural travel → Nakhon Si Thammarat or Songkhla; business → Hat Yai or Nakhon Si Thammarat. Sandbox Hotel in Nakhon Si Thammarat is ideal for cultural and business travel.

What is Southern Thailand known for?

Islands and beaches (Phuket, Phi Phi, Koh Samui), the Andaman Sea and Gulf of Thailand coastlines, Buddhist temples, Muslim cultural heritage in the deep south, Thai-Malay cuisine, and historic cities like Nakhon Si Thammarat and Songkhla.

What is the best time to visit Southern Thailand?

The two coastlines have opposite wet seasons. Gulf Coast (Nakhon Si Thammarat, Koh Samui): best Feb–Sep. Andaman Coast (Phuket, Krabi): best Nov–Apr. If travelling outside these windows, check the specific weather pattern for your destination.

Is Nakhon Si Thammarat in Southern Thailand?

Yes. Nakhon Si Thammarat is one of the largest and most historically significant cities in Southern Thailand, located on the Gulf Coast. Sandbox Hotel is based here.
