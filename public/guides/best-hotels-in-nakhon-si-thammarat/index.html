About Nakhon Si Thammarat

Nakhon Si Thammarat (นครศรีธรรมราช) is the capital of Nakhon Si Thammarat Province, situated on the Gulf of Thailand coast in Southern Thailand approximately 780 km south of Bangkok. The city has over 1,000 years of recorded history and is home to some of Thailand's most significant Buddhist monuments.

Key reasons people visit

  • Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan — one of Thailand's most revered Buddhist temple complexes, with a central chedi said to contain a tooth relic of the Buddha
  • Nakhon Si Thammarat National Museum — prehistoric artefacts, Hindu-Buddhist sculpture, and shadow puppet displays
  • Nang Talung — the living shadow puppet theatre tradition unique to Nakhon Si Thammarat
  • Southern Thai food — khao yam, gaeng tai pla, and other dishes not found elsewhere in Thailand
  • Gateway to national parks — Khao Luang, Nam Tok Yong, and Gulf Coast beaches at Khanom and Sichon
