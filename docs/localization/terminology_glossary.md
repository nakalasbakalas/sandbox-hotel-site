# Localization Terminology Glossary

**Purpose**: Consistent translation and transcreation of key terms across all locales.

**Date**: 2026-03-18
**Languages**: English (en), Thai (th), Simplified Chinese (zh-CN)
**Project**: Sandbox Hotel Website

---

## HOW TO USE THIS GLOSSARY

1. **For consistent terms** (product names, features): Use the exact translation provided
2. **For adaptable terms** (marketing phrases): Use the spirit/intent, adapt as needed for natural phrasing
3. **For proper nouns**: Follow the transliteration/handling guidance

**Legend**:
- 🔒 = **Locked** (use exact term, no variation)
- 🎯 = **Flexible** (adapt phrasing naturally while preserving intent)
- 📍 = **Proper noun** (follow handling guidance)

## SPECIAL NOTES

### Thai Language Considerations:
- **Politeness particles**: Use "ครับ/ค่ะ" in formal communication (FAQ answers, booking confirmations)
- **Classifiers**: Use appropriate classifiers (ห้อง for rooms, คืน for nights, ท่าน/คน for guests)
- **Register**: Mix of Thai and transliterated English is natural for modern hospitality (e.g., "Standard Double")

### Chinese Language Considerations:
- **Simplified vs. Traditional**: This glossary uses **Simplified Chinese** (大陆简体中文) targeting mainland China visitors
- **Formality**: Use 您 (formal "you") in direct address contexts; avoid overly casual 你
- **Measure words**: Use appropriate measure words (间 for rooms, 位 for guests, 晚 for nights)
- **Place names**: For Thai place names, use established transliterations when available (洛坤府 for Nakhon Si Thammarat)

### Transcreation Principles:
- **🔒 Locked terms**: Consistency critical for product names, specs, and legal terms
- **🎯 Flexible terms**: Adapt for natural phrasing while keeping the persuasive intent
- **📍 Proper nouns**: Follow local conventions for place names and brands
